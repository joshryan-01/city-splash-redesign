Who we are

A space to unite, connect and create the future

City Splash is a 30,000-capacity festival that celebrates Caribbean and African culture: reggae, dancehall, jungle, garage, afrobeats and amapiano, across six stages in Brockwell Park. Now in its seventh year, it's grown from a single-day celebration into a movement: a space for the community to unite, connect and build what comes next.

01

The Music

Reggae, dancehall and afrobeats aren't just genres on a bill. They're culturally significant sounds that have shaped youth culture and lifestyle for decades. City Splash puts the heritage acts who built that sound on the same stages as the artists breaking through right now.

02

The Food

In partnership with Black Eats LDN, around 40 of London's top Black-owned Caribbean and African street traders set up a market village on site, because the food is as much a part of the culture as the music.

03

The Future

Beyond the day itself, City Splash champions Black arts, fashion, beauty and craft through vendor partnerships, and invests directly in new talent through programmes like Rise Up.
